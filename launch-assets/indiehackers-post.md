## Post Title
From frustration to 2,000 users: how I built a video transcription tool around one privacy insight

## Body

A year ago I was building something unrelated and kept needing to transcribe videos. I tried every tool I could find and kept hitting the same two walls:

**Wall 1: Speed.** Every tool I tried wanted 5–15 minutes per video. If you're transcribing 10 videos a week, that's an hour of just... waiting.

**Wall 2: Storage.** I read the privacy policies. Almost every tool keeps your uploaded video on their servers. One said "up to 90 days." Another had no deletion policy at all. If you're working with client footage or confidential recordings, that's genuinely a problem.

So I built VideoText to solve both.

---

**The product:**

Seven tools in one platform:
1. Video → Transcript (speakers, summary, chapters, keywords)
2. Video → Subtitles (SRT/VTT)
3. Translate Subtitles (50+ languages)
4. Fix Subtitles (timing, fillers, line breaks)
5. Burn Subtitles into video
6. Compress Video
7. Batch Process (up to 100 videos, ZIP export)

Average processing time: under 60 seconds.
File lifecycle: deleted immediately after the job completes.

---

**What's worked:**

1. **Free tier without a credit card.** 3 imports/month. This alone drives most signups — people want to trust the product before they pay. Let them.

2. **Leading with privacy as a feature, not a policy.** "We don't store your data" is in the hero, on the pricing page, in the FAQ, in every Reddit post. It's become the thing people remember and share. Agencies especially — they can't legally upload client footage to a tool with indefinite storage.

3. **SEO from day 1.** 28+ long-tail pages targeting searches like "mp4 to srt", "subtitle timing fixer", "meeting transcript generator". Organic traffic is compounding slowly but consistently.

4. **Pricing at the right ceiling.** Free → Basic ($19) → Pro ($49) → Agency ($129). The jump from Free to Basic is the hardest. I made it easier by giving the free tier real value (not crippled), so when people hit the 3-import limit, they've already seen the tool work.

---

**What I'd do differently:**

- Validate the core tool (transcript) much earlier before building 6 more
- Launch on PH and HN in month 2, not month 6
- Build the email list from day 1 — I left a lot of "interested users" on the table by not capturing them early

---

**Current numbers:**
- 2,000+ active creators
- 2M+ minutes transcribed
- 98.5% accuracy
- 50+ languages
